# ANOTHER WAY

## The premise

It feels like there are only two options. Yell, or swallow it. Give up, or push harder. Decide right now — or it'll be "too late." If you don't deceive, you'll be deceived. The lie is already baked into the equation.

Something tears at you — *what do I do?* — and in that moment you can't see the whole picture. A boss leans on you for overtime through guilt and quietly devalues your work. Society installed the program long ago: trust no one, you'll get burned — so you start deceiving too, certain there's no other way.

A website flashes a countdown on the discount. An influencer shouts that only two seats are left, and the next launch is in a year — or never. *Decide now!* Manufactured urgency, fear of missing out — and your real interest is already trampled; all that's left is someone else's hunger to push you into a fear-purchase. Your inner compass jerks toward "the pill" — because you're small, and the "expert" is big and "respected."

The lie is hidden right here. And the real you is nowhere in it.

It's almost always like this: the lie lives in the framing itself. And there's almost always a third move — **a third door** — you can't see from inside your own point of view. It opens the moment you step outside the choice you were handed and notice the assumption that was slipped in without a word.

There's always another way.

That third move is what we work on here. Live.

## What we actually do

You bring a real knot from your work with people — a conflict with your boss, a team that won't move, a promotion that keeps slipping, a partner you can't reach. Out loud, in the room.

Together we find the lie in the framing — and you leave with **one concrete step you can take Monday morning**. Not inspiration. A move.

Every meeting is a chance to see a new facet of yourself — where you betray yourself, where you take things on faith and pay for it later. And right there, a live rehearsal: a space to try on, without fear, a braver version of yourself — the one who isn't afraid to say no, who spots manipulation and calmly shuts it down.

The format is roughly half talk, half practice: short input, work in pairs and small groups, and live breakdowns of real situations on the chair. You watch one person's breakdown and take from it whatever fits your own life — that's how the room works.

## Who this is for

People who work with people, on both sides of the table:

- **Those who lead** — founders, owners, managers — and feel the team isn't moving, or isn't with them.
- **Those who are led** — specialists, remote workers, builders — who do everything right and still feel unseen or stuck.

Both see only part of the picture. The friction lives exactly where those two pictures collide. That collision is our whole subject.

And it's a gathering of people who are tired of pretending and bending to others' expectations — who sincerely look for answers and do the work on themselves.

## What we value

- **Honesty** over comfort. We'd rather tell you the truth, kindly, than keep things pleasant.
- **Vulnerability** — you go as deep as *you* choose to go.
- **Psychological safety** — non-negotiable. What's said in the room stays in the room.
- **Action over insight-collecting** — we close every breakdown with something you actually do.
- **Your own pace, your own path** — this is an invitation to walk alongside, not to follow anyone.

## What we practice

Two things we ask of each other in the room:

- **You're responsible for the move, not the outcome.** You take the step; you don't control how it lands. Old wisdom, still true: *you have a right to your actions, never to their fruits* (Bhagavad Gita 2.47). Make the move, release the result — that's where the weight comes off.
- **Leave your expectations at the door.** Come without a verdict on how the evening should go, or who anyone should become — including yourself. The work goes deepest when no one is gripping the result.

## What this is not

Not a lecture series. Not therapy. Not a guru and his followers. No one here will tell you The Answer — the best breakdowns end with *you* saying the thing, in your own words.

## The core

This is a community where I'm calling you to leadership. Leadership of your own life.

To honesty with yourself. To accepting your own vulnerability — and to stop hiding it, even from yourself.

I start with me. I'm not perfect, and I get things wrong. More than that — my whole path is made of mistakes, some of which still sting with shame. I'm not telling you this so you'll admire my courage. I'm telling you to lower the bar: if I can do this, so can you. You don't have to arrive polished here. You have to arrive real.

It genuinely scares me to step out and make this the core of a community. But it's my choice. And if it resonates with you — this is your place.

Leadership of your own life starts with one thing: stop waiting for someone big and "respected" to decide for you. Take your own wheel.

Come to work on yourself, not to watch.
