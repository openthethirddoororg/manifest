# How we work together — the rules

Two layers, two sets of rules. The **community** is open. The **breakdown rooms** are closed and held tight — that's exactly what makes them safe enough to go deep.

---

## A. In the breakdown room (live meetings)

These rules are firm. They exist to protect the person who opened up.

1. **Signed up — show up.** Seats are few (the first rooms are ~10 people). If you signed up, you'll be there. The room runs on a stable group.
2. **Confidentiality is absolute.** What's said in the room stays in the room. No names, no stories outside. This is the price of safety.
3. **You come to work, not to watch.** There's no "passive observer" seat. You choose how deep you go into *your* work — but you don't come to gawk at someone else's. Most of the time is practice, not listening.
4. **Bring a real knot.** A concrete situation — a conflict, a dead end — not an abstract question. The more real, the better the work.
5. **You'll get the truth, directly.** Honestly, without cruelty. If you don't want that right now, this isn't your room yet.
6. **Phones away.** Be in the room.
7. **The host holds safety.** The host protects the room gently but firmly — and will remove anyone who breaks safety. That protection is their job, not hostility.

Break confidentiality or safety, and you leave. No exceptions.

---

## B. In the community (WhatsApp and everywhere outside)

Lighter, but real. The spirit here is mutual help.

1. **Help others.** Share what you know, answer when you can. This is a place to lift, not to shine.
2. **Share materials freely** — books, tools, notes, frameworks. Credit the source.
3. **No selling, spam, or recruiting.** Not your business, not your beliefs. If you have something genuinely useful, ask the host first.
4. **Confidentiality carries over here too.** Don't retell what participants share — not here, not anywhere.
5. **Disagree with respect.** Push on ideas, not on people. "Another way" applies to how we argue, too.
6. **Keep the tone simple and human.** No walls of jargon, no posturing.

---

## How the rules change

The rules aren't sacred — but they aren't rewritten in the middle of a meeting.

See a conflict or a flaw in a rule? Raise it **between meetings**: a message to the host or an issue in the repository. We review and revise consciously, not in the heat of a breakdown. Openness to feedback here is real; turning the hot seat into a debate about the rules is not.
