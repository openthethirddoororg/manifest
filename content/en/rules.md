# Rules

These rules exist to create a space where it isn't scary to step into vulnerability — to open up about what you sometimes have no one to discuss at work, or conflicts in life.

1. **Confidentiality.** What's said in the room stays in the room. No names or stories go outside. Only a group photo, if you want one.
2. **You come intending to work actively.** You choose how deep you go into *your* work. Most of the time is practice, not listening.
3. **If you're ready — bring your case.** A concrete situation — a conflict, a dead end, something you ran into yourself, something that worries you. We'll work through it so you come away seeing what other ways of acting are available.
4. **You'll get the truth, directly.** Honestly, without cruelty. If you don't want that right now, this isn't your room yet.
5. **Phones away.** Be in the room.
6. **The host holds safety.** The host protects the room gently but firmly — and will remove anyone who breaks safety. That protection is their job, not hostility.

Break confidentiality or safety, and you leave. No exceptions.

---

## How the rules change

The rules aren't sacred — but they aren't rewritten in the middle of a meeting.

See a conflict or a flaw in a rule? Raise it **between meetings**: a message to the host or an [issue in the repository](https://github.com/openthethirddoororg/manifest/issues). We review and revise consciously, not in the heat of a breakdown. Openness to feedback here is real; turning the hot seat into a debate about the rules is not.
