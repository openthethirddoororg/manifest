# Code of Conduct

Psychological safety is not negotiable here. This code is the same one we live by
in the room, written down. It applies to every space connected to this community —
live meetings, the community chat, this repository, and anywhere we represent the
group.

## The spirit

This is a place to work on yourself, not to perform. We'd rather tell each other
the truth, kindly, than keep things comfortable and false. We come as we are —
imperfect, sometimes stuck — and we treat that as the starting point, not a
failing.

## What we expect

- **Keep it safe.** People open up here. Protect that. No mockery, no contempt,
  no using someone's openness against them.
- **Confidentiality.** What's said in the room stays in the room. Don't repeat
  names or stories outside — not in chat, not anywhere. This is the price of
  safety, and it's absolute.
- **Honesty, with care.** Speak the truth as you see it, plainly, without cruelty.
  Push on ideas, never on people.
- **Dignity is equal.** Owner or employee, expert or newcomer — status stays at
  the door. We serve the person in front of us; we don't perform at them.
- **No selling, spam, or recruiting.** Not your business, not your beliefs, not
  your worldview. If you have something genuinely useful to share, ask the host
  first.
- **Your pace, your path.** No one is pushed deeper than they choose. This is an
  invitation to walk alongside, not pressure to follow.

## What's not acceptable

- Breaking confidentiality.
- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks in any form.
- Making the space unsafe for someone who opened up.
- Using the community to sell, recruit, or push an agenda after being asked to
  stop.

## Enforcement

The host holds the container. They will, gently but firmly, protect the space —
including asking someone to leave. In live rooms, breaking confidentiality or
safety means leaving, without exception. In online spaces, the host may warn,
remove content, or remove a person as needed.

This protection is the host's job, not hostility.

## Reporting & changing the rules

If something here is unsafe, or you see a flaw in these rules, raise it **between
meetings** — message the host or open an issue in this repository. We revise
consciously, not in the heat of a session. Openness to feedback here is real;
turning the hot seat into a debate about the rules is not.
